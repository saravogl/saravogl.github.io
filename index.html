aboutme

Hey, it’s me. Sara without an ‘h’. I’m not sure how you found your way to my page, but I’m glad you’re here!

If you already know a thing or two about me, feel free to skip ahead. For those of you who I’ve never met, let’s take a minute to get virtually acquainted, shall we?

First things first: I’m *really* proud to say I built this site from the ground up. No template, no WordPress, no Squarespace -- just good ‘ole HTML, CSS, jQuery, and JavaScript. It’s still a work in progress, so feel free to let me know your thoughts -- the good, the bad, and the ugly. (I grew up with a brother so I know how to take a punch…or two, or three, or four.)

Speaking of growing up: I was raised in Michigan and found my way to Chicago by way of Loyola University Chicago (go Ramblers!). I’ve always been a lover of people, places, and ideas, so it didn’t take long for Chicago to feel like home to me. This city is full of amazing people, and I want to try get to know (and learn from) as many of them as possible.

I’m a huge believer in seeking-out challenges, trusting your instincts, and letting that space between where you are and where you want to be inspire and motivate you to do big things.

What about you?

lifework

Growing up, I was always jealous of those people who knew *exactly* what they wanted to be when they grew up. Sure, not all of my friends realized their dreams of becoming astronauts or the President of the United States, but a lot of my classmates did grow up to be doctors, teachers, firemen, nurses, and scientists -- which is incredible, when you think about it. Me? I'm still one of the many trying to figure out what I want to be when I grow up -- but I try not to see that as a weakness; it's an opportunity to try new things, isn't it? At this point in my life, I define success by taking advantage of the opportunity to learn as much as I can at my job, taking initiative and seeking out new education opportunities (like the rad General Assembly course that helped me build this site), and constantly challenging myself to think creatively and unconventionally.

Now that you know a bit about me, I have to confess that I am currently living a double-life -- and I love it. By-day I’m a Director of Client Services for an enrollment and marketing services company, and by night I’m a Cardio Dance instructor at various studios and gyms around the city. Interested in talking business? Say hello. Want to dance it out together? Come join me at a class. We can even combine both options, if you're into it.

Oh! Take a look at my resume if you have a minute.

let'sdance

As a dance instructor, my goal is to help women (and men!) feel confident and comfortable in their own skin. This is something we all struggle with on a daily basis and the best way that I know to combat these negative thoughts is in the studio. You’d be surprised how much your attitude, stress-level, and mindset can change in 60-minutes when the music is blasting and you’re booty-shakin’ with a bunch of kick-ass women. Don’t believe me?
